# Research Prioritization Model

## Purpose

Phase 19 adds a bounded research prioritization engine that surfaces where the Observatory should focus next without turning structural gaps into causal claims or certainty theater.

## What Prioritization Is

- deterministic ranking of research gaps from persisted evidence and replay/calibration state
- advisory-only guidance reused across briefing, dossier, timeline, export, and dedicated priority routes
- a way to highlight where missing evidence, shallow empirical depth, limited calibration, or weak comparison support still constrain interpretation

## What Prioritization Is Not

- proof that an experiment is correct
- proof that a comparative leader is causally stronger
- a hidden write-side decision system
- a claim that replay maturity implies exact historical reconstruction

## Priority Classes

- `evidence-deficient-but-promising`
- `replay-mature-but-empirically-shallow`
- `empirically-strong-but-calibration-limited`
- `comparison-limited`
- `saturation-emerging`

## Anti-Drift Rules

- Prioritization ranks structural gaps only.
- Priority labels stay advisory and bounded.
- GET routes remain observational and do not persist priority state.
- Exported priority summaries remain descriptive metadata, not action commitments.
- Full replay remains artifact-complete lineage, not exact historical certainty.
