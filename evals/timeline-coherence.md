# Timeline Coherence

> Phase 11 evaluation criteria for comparative research timeline playback.

---

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the timeline layer produces a readable, deterministic account of observatory evolution without inventing history.

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

1. Chronological integrity
The session order must match persisted timestamps with no reordering drift.

2. Session readability
Each timeline session should communicate objective, preferred strategy, blocker state, and evidence posture without excess verbosity.

3. World-state honesty
Timeline world-state commentary must never imply historical diffs that were not actually persisted.

4. Event narrative restraint
Narrative playback should read as a lab log, not speculative storytelling.

5. Cross-surface consistency
Timeline summaries must remain consistent with research memory, transition patterns, and briefing surfaces.

6. Empty-state integrity
Sparse timelines must degrade honestly when sessions, shifts, or calibration points are absent.
